Parijat (1937-1993)


Parijat, the pseudonym of Bishnu Kumari Waiba, stands as a towering figure in Nepali literature, her impact resonating through time. Despite battling rheumatoid arthritis, she crafted poignant narratives that capture the essence of human emotion amidst Nepal's societal transformation. Her seminal work, "Shirishko Phool" (The Blue Mimosa), explores the intricacies of love, loss, and resilience, reflecting the zeitgeist of her era. Parijat's poetic verses, infused with themes of nature and spirituality, evoke a sense of timeless wonder and introspection. Honored with prestigious awards like the Madan Puraskar, her literary legacy endures as a beacon of inspiration for aspiring writers and readers alike. Through her words, Parijat transcends the boundaries of language and culture, touching the hearts and minds of individuals across generations, cementing her status as one of Nepal's most cherished literary luminaries.


Awards won by Parijat

  • Sajha Puraskar
  • Madan Puraskar
  • Yubak Griha Award
